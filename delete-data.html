Request Data Deletion — StepMatch

App: StepMatch  ·  Developer: Platpuff  ·  Package: com.platpuff.stepmatch  ·  Last updated: 2026-06-11

This page explains how to request deletion of data that StepMatch (developed by Platpuff) holds about you, what gets deleted, what is retained, and how long that takes.

How to request deletion

You have two options. The first is instant and self-serve; the second covers anything held on our servers.

Option 1 — Delete your local data instantly (no email needed)

On your Android device:
  1. Open the device Settings app.
  2. Go to Apps (or Apps & notifications).
  3. Find and tap StepMatch.
  4. Tap Storage & cache → Clear storage (sometimes called "Clear data").

This immediately erases all locally-stored game progress, settings, cached step counter watermarks, pet state, shop inventory, and Firebase Analytics state. You can also achieve the same result by uninstalling the app.

Option 2 — Request deletion of server-side data via email

Some game data — your anonymous leaderboard entry, your linked-Google friend list (if you opted in), and any challenge records — lives on our servers in Firebase. To request that this data be deleted, send us an email:

Email: stepmatch97@gmail.com
Subject line: StepMatch Data Deletion Request

Include in the email:

  • The Google account email you used (only if you ever signed in with Google — anonymous players can skip this).
  • Your in-game username, if any.
  • Whether you want all of your server-side data deleted, or only a specific kind (for example, "delete my leaderboard entry only").

We respond to requests within 30 days, and the deletion itself is typically completed within 14 days of receipt (often within 48 hours).

What we cannot delete

The following are outside our control:

  • Google Play Store records — install/uninstall/review/rating data is held by Google, not by us.
  • Backups already created by Android Auto Backup or device-to-device transfer — these are managed by Google / your device manufacturer.
  • Aggregated, anonymized analytics — once individual events are aggregated into counters that cannot be tied back to a specific install, that aggregated data remains.
